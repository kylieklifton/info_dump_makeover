We Need to Talk About Who "Owns" the Planet

You've probably seen infographics like the one above. Neat little slices showing which countries are the "biggest." Russia: 3.34%. Canada: 1.96%. Very tidy.

But these numbers hide more than they reveal. Let's actually look at what we're claiming—and what that means.

Everything We Fight Over

All the land. All the borders. Every war, every treaty, every "this is mine" moment in human history—it amounts to less than a third of the planet's surface.

The "Lawless" Majority

And here's the part no one talks about: the largest single chunk of Earth—43%—belongs to no one. International waters. The high seas. "Lawless," we call it.

Chart 3: International waters

But "lawless" really means "not yet carved up." Deep-sea mining, unregulated fishing, plastic dumping—this is the next frontier of exploitation. And powerful nations are already positioning themselves.
